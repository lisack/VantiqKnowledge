**Summary:**  
Vantiq is a versatile platform that allows developers to create a wide range of applications, from emergency response systems to AI-driven health monitoring. This video provides an overview of the tools available on the Vantiq platform, which include a Visual Integrated Development Environment, AI assistance, and various low-code program logic builder tools. Developers can build both the backend services and frontend user interfaces using prebuilt widgets or custom code. Vantiq supports easy integration with internet-connected devices and services, and offers flexible deployment options from cloud to local devices. For more information, visit community.Vantiq.com.

**Original Content:**  
Vantiq is a platform that lets developers build, well, just about anything.

Emergency first response systems?

Check.

Smart buildings?

Check.

Irrigation systems?

Solar panel controls?

AI driven health monitoring?

You can build them all in Vantiq.

These possibilities and more make for great reasons to answer why Vantiq, But this video starting your development training journey seeks to give you a glimpse into how Vantiq.

By how Vantiq we mean, of course, what are the tools on the platform the developers use to make all these high performance, situationally aware, automated and AI agentic systems?

Here's the 10,000 foot fly over of them.

Developers do their work within a namespace, isolated securely from other work using the Visual Integrated Development environment.

Here.

Handy menus make all the tools and project resources readily available for use.

Help in the form of AI assistance, documentation, and technical training are also available at the click of a button.

The Design Model tool lets developers describe what their application should be in natural language and generates the framework and many of the resources needed to develop it.

Services are collections of logic, program, state, testing and debugging tools, as well as other related resources that build the back end of any application.

They have defined inputs and outputs with processing inside.

Through these inputs and outputs, they interact with outside entities, other services, and other tools on the platform.

If services are defined as agents, more abilities are unlocked for AI runtime decision making in the service.

Within services, there are two types of low code program logic builder tools.

Visual event handlers generally process data messages, otherwise known as events, that come into the service.

A number of predefined code patterns are visually linked together into application flows that can manipulate the data, perform statistical analysis, or be evaluated by AI.

The other builder is for generative AI procedures.

The predefined code patterns here are for creating procedural flows for complex AI based logic.

Clients are the front end user interfaces.

Developers drag in prebuilt widgets onto the client canvas and codelessly connect them to data or AI for display, or they can build their own in JavaScript, HTML, and CSS.

The Vantiq platform integrates easily with anything on the Internet, even if there's a firewall in between.

A variety of prebuilt tools facilitate connections to anything from camera feeds, message and AI protocols, databases, API's, microservices, and so on.

Among those tools are software development kits and a React Native library for mobile device connections.

Vantiq even has its own mobile app available for free from the Google Play and Apple stores.

Finally, let's talk about application distribution and deployment.

Vantiq servers can deploy in the cloud, a single laptop, or anything in between.

And yes, there are tools to make that a single button action.

Developers can codelessly set up node and catalog connections to share resources and processing for optimal performance and scalability.

In short, anything you want to build.
