Service Sources In this video, we'll discuss how Vantiq integrates with outside entities.

Vantiq is an event driven platform, and sources are what bring those events into the platform.

Sources can connect anything on the Internet, from camera feeds flagged by object recognition software to simple sensors, to applications program interfaces provided by web services.

Even legacy systems and others that don't push events can be pulled to pull them in.

We also have a mobile app so people themselves can interact with Vantiq applications.

Vantiq has predefined source configurations based on specific messaging protocols and online web services like MQTT or an API.

These are our built in sources.

To use them you'll need little more than their Internet location and maybe your credentials.

Other sources called Enterprise Connectors or Extension sources are hosted on Vantiq GitHub website and they greatly facilitate the making of connections with more specialized entities like JDBC databases and object recognition camera feeds.

Still another way of connecting to 3rd party entities is through Vantiq Camel assemblies, available from a public catalog.

These can be used to connect dozens of different popular web entities, with more being added periodically.

The Vantiq Academy integration class has details and examples on how to connect reliably to outside entities in your labs.
