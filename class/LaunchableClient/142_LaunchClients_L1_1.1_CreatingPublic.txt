We'll quickly explain what makes public clients public, then how to designate them so in the Client Builder, Public clients are Vantiq applications made available to users who do not have Vantiq logins.

The take away from the demonstration you saw is that you won't know if your public clients are truly public unless you test them in a window where the browser conceals your identity.

Just because your client is public doesn't mean that we can ignore the security of the application.

That security happens in two ways.

First, images in the client must be designated as public images.

Second, the only point of connection between the client and Vantiq server takes place through public procedures.

Let's look at each of these important restrictions in turn.

Any kind of image in a public client will only show up in the browser window if it's in the document public subdirectory.

This includes image widgets, background URL's, etcetera.

Also, clients can only execute public procedures.

These are procedures flagged ARS_Public equals true.

Clients can't work directly with anything else.

Not service events and data streams, not sources, not standard types.

The good news is that public procedures can access all of these back end resources.

So while security is maintained by having the procedures specify the conditions, clients can get second hand access to what they need.
